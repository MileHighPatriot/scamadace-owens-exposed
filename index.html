How we rank evidence (read this before the claims)

Every disproof item on this site carries a tier badge. Higher tiers are harder evidence. Candace Owens’s show confidence is never treated as Tier 1. Analysis is useful — and always labeled as analysis.

Tier 1

Court filings, charging presentations, and case exhibits described in open proceedings

What a prosecutor puts on the record in charging documents or walks through in preliminary hearings — especially physical exhibits (weapon class, ballistic discussion, DNA on a specific part of a firearm), authenticated communications offered as evidence, and other materials subject to adversarial testing. We use Tier 1 only when it directly collides with the claim (for example: firearm exhibits vs. “exploding microphone,” or travel records in a defamation complaint vs. a Fort Huachuca meeting story). We do not paste the whole case onto every page as filler.

Example: Open-court discussion of a rifle and trigger DNA is Tier 1 against “no gun / mic bomb,” because it is the wrong weapon class for that theory.

Tier 2

Named officials and on-the-record law-enforcement / government briefings

Statements attributable to named officials, agencies, or formal press briefings about scene geometry, timeline, wound description, manhunt facts, or investigative posture. This is weaker than a court exhibit but stronger than pundit commentary because it is institutional and on the record.

Example: Official descriptions of a distant rooftop shot to the neck are Tier 2 against trap-door / under-stage theories.

Tier 3

Primary video and audio of the event

Multi-angle phone and media recordings of the courtyard speech, the shot, and the immediate aftermath. Primary media is often the fastest disproof of mechanism fantasies (exploding mic, no gunshot, ground-origin blast) because the public can pause the same frames. Theories that require you to disbelieve the tape need an extraordinary reason.

Example: A gunshot report synchronized with a neck wound on multi-angle video is Tier 3 against “no gun was ever fired” paired with a mic-explosion story.

Tier 4

Multi-outlet reporting that documents documents, data errors, or primary posts

Careful reporting that quotes charging presentations, reproduces Owens’s primary posts, or documents checkable errors (such as reading UTC as Utah local time on flight data). We prefer outlets that point back to primary material. Secondary reporting is never as good as the underlying exhibit — but it is how most people can verify what was said and what failed.

Example: Reporting that Owens treated a UTC departure stamp as local Utah time is Tier 4 against the “Egyptian plane fled after the hit” line — because the arithmetic is independently checkable.

Tier 5

Analysis, logic, method critiques, and “how to pin the claim”

Explicit reasoning: unfalsifiable patterns, base rates, free-association (maroon berets, “33,” bees), anonymous-source problems, and practical ways to force a claimant to produce a document or measurement. Tier 5 is often the most useful for arguing with followers — and it is always labeled so nobody confuses it with a lab report. Podcast certainty from Owens is also effectively Tier 5-or-below; it is not evidence that she is right.

Example: Explaining why “denial = guilt” is an unfalsifiable trap is Tier 5 analysis — useful, but not a physical exhibit.

How to read a claim page: Her claim first → where she said it → then the evidence stack from hard collision facts down to method analysis. If two tiers conflict, prefer the higher tier. If something is still unknown, unknown is not proof of her theory.
